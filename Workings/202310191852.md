$$
\begin{align}
\text{Let } A=
\begin{pmatrix}
2 & 3 & 3 \\
0 & -2 & 1 \\
-2 & 0 & 1
\end{pmatrix}
\end{align}
$$
Prove that $A^3-A^2 +2A+22I=0$

First we find $A^2$ :

$$
\begin{align}

\begin{bmatrix}
2 & 3 & 3 \\
0 & -2 & 1 \\
-2 & 0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
\times
\begin{bmatrix}
2 & 3 & 3 \\
0 & -2 & 1 \\
-2 & 0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
\end{align}
$$
---
$$
\begin{align}
\begin{bmatrix}
2&3&3
\end{bmatrix}
\cdot 
\begin{bmatrix}
2\\
0\\
-2
\end{bmatrix}\\\\
\text{thus :    } 
(2 \times 2) + (3 \times 0) + (3 \times -2) \\\\
A^2_{11}=-2 
\end{align}
$$

---
$$
\begin{align}
\begin{bmatrix}
2&3&3
\end{bmatrix}
\cdot 
\begin{bmatrix}
3\\
-2\\
0
\end{bmatrix}\\\\
\text{thus : } (2 \times 3) + (3\times-2) + (3\times 0) \\\\
A^2_{12}= 0 \\ \\
\end{align}
$$

$$
\begin{align}
\begin{bmatrix}

\end{bmatrix}

\begin{bmatrix}

1
\end{bmatrix}\\\\
\text{thus : } (2 \times 3) + (3\times1) + (3\times 1) \\\\
A^2_{13}= 12 \\ \\
\end{align}
$$

$$
